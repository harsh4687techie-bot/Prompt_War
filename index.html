Common Questions

Frequently Asked

Quick answers to common election questions.

How do I register to vote?

You can register to vote online through your state's election website, by mail, or in person at your local election office. Requirements vary by state, but generally you need to be a U.S. citizen, be 18 years old by Election Day, and meet your state's residency requirements. Our AI assistant can guide you through the specific steps for your state.

What documents do I need to vote?

Requirements vary by state. Common forms of voter ID include a driver's license, state-issued ID, passport, or military ID. Some states also accept utility bills, bank statements, or other official documents. Use our assistant to find the exact requirements for your state.

Can I vote if I recently moved?

Yes! If you've moved within the same state, you typically need to update your voter registration with your new address. If you've moved to a new state, you'll need to register in your new state. Many states allow same-day registration. Check your state's deadlines to make sure you're registered in time.

What is early voting and absentee voting?

Early voting allows you to cast your ballot before Election Day at designated locations. Absentee voting (vote by mail) lets you mail in your ballot if you can't make it to the polls. Availability and rules vary by state. Our assistant can help you explore these options based on your location.

Is my information safe with VoteGuide?

Absolutely. VoteGuide stores your journey progress locally on your device using your browser's localStorage. We never collect, store, or transmit your personal information to any server. Your privacy is our priority.
